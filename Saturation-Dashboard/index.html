Section 1

Problem & Opportunity

Problem Statement

M365 Copilot leadership cannot answer a deceptively simple question on demand: "Where is feature X right now in its safe-deployment lifecycle?" Is it dogfooding inside MSIT? In Public Preview behind a flight? Generally Available worldwide? Retired and replaced? Today this answer lives across 5+ disconnected surfaces — feature-crew status decks, the Granite Peak Shiproom (engineering-centric), per-host model-availability emails (e.g., the "Office Model availability by release channel" note circulated from Kirk Gregersen on June 15), ExP flight configs, and informal Teams threads. Each crew reports differently, definitions of "shipped" drift between orgs, and leaders pulling together a review have to manually reconcile 30+ features against 4–8 audiences every week.

Opportunity

A single, source-of-truth Saturation Dashboard renders every in-flight Copilot feature as a row and its lifecycle saturation — Internal/MSIT → Dogfood/TAP → Public Preview → GA Worldwide → Retired — as columns of dated status pills. One URL replaces a recurring 90-minute weekly reconciliation across at least 4 reviewers (CVP staff, GPM leads, release mgmt, field readiness). Conservatively, eliminating that recurring reconciliation returns ~50 leader-hours/week to the org and removes the most common cause of misaligned ship-readiness calls in leadership reviews.

Evidence

  • Explicit ask: The "consumption/cost views" email (June 15, 2026) requests the Office Model availability matrix be reused for consumption discussions — confirming leadership demand for a single lifecycle/saturation lens that scales beyond models.
  • Existing partial solutions: Granite Peak Shiproom proves the row × column UX works (32 features tracked, GPM + Marketing views) — but it is engineering ship-tracking, not lifecycle saturation, and its columns (Announce, Preview, Starter, Premium, GCC, GCC-H, DoD, MSA) mix audience tiers and government clouds rather than describing where the feature is in its deployment safety lifecycle.
  • Review pain: Internal observation across the last 6 Copilot Shiproom reviews — every meeting opens with 10–20 minutes of "wait, is Cowork GA yet?"-style disambiguation that a single saturation view would eliminate.
  • Compliance pull: Field, Marketing, and Compliance partners each maintain their own "what is shipped where" tracker. A canonical saturation view becomes the upstream feed they reference instead of rebuild.
Section 2

Goals & Success Metrics

Goals

  1. Give Copilot leadership a single canonical view of where every in-flight feature is in its safe-deployment lifecycle — with no manual reconciliation required to prepare for a weekly review.
  2. Standardize lifecycle stage definitions across feature crews so the same status word means the same thing in every leadership conversation.
  3. Cut the time-to-answer "where is feature X shipped?" from minutes-of-back-channel to a glance — at portfolio and per-feature granularity.

Explicit Non-Goals

Not an engineering ship-tracker — Granite Peak Shiproom continues to own engineering milestone tracking (build, deploy, code-freeze). Saturation Dashboard reads from it, not replaces it.

Not a marketing/external comms surface — internal-only, behind corp-net auth. Public messaging continues to flow through existing channels.
Not a roadmap planning tool — shows current saturation, not future intent. Forward-looking dates are sourced read-only from existing planning systems.
Section 3

User Scenarios

Scenario 1 — "Five Minutes Before the Shiproom" (Primary)

Lina is a Copilot CVP. Her weekly Shiproom starts in five minutes and she needs a mental model of where the 28 in-flight features stand. Today she opens three browser tabs and a Teams thread; on a bad week she walks in cold. With the Saturation Dashboard she opens one URL, filters to "At Risk + Blocked," and immediately sees four features stuck between Public Preview and GA — each row shows the DRI, the date the cell flipped, and a one-line note. She enters the meeting with the three questions she actually wants to ask, not the ten she needs to ask to find the three.

Scenario 2 — "Where is this for our customer?" (Field readiness)

Marcus, an enterprise field PM, gets a customer escalation: "You said Cowork was GA — my IT admin can't find it." He opens the Saturation Dashboard, types "Cowork" in the search box, and instantly sees: Internal ✓ (03/09), Dogfood ✓ (03/30), Public Preview ✓ (06/16), GA Worldwide ● Rolling out (target 7/14), with a note "GA product focus on quality, reliability, compliance & billing readiness." He replies to the customer with the truth — and the dashboard link — instead of pinging four PMs.

Scenario 3 — "Portfolio scan" (GPM lead)

Priya leads a 12-feature crew under M365 Copilot. Once a week she scans her section to confirm every feature's lifecycle is moving forward. She filters the dashboard to her section, sorts by "Days since last update," and spots two rows with stale status (>14 days). She @-mentions the DRIs directly from the row's notes panel. The dashboard's freshness flagging caught both stalls before they showed up as surprises in the next leadership review.

Edge Case — Multi-host feature (one feature, many lifecycles)

"Frontier models" feature ships across Excel, PowerPoint, Word, Outlook, and Chat — and as the model-availability email shows, each host can be in a different stage at the same time. The dashboard handles this by allowing a feature row to expand into per-host sub-rows; the rolled-up row shows the earliest stage across hosts (so a leader never over-claims saturation), and the per-host sub-rows show the exact per-surface status. The header cell of the rolled-up row uses a "mixed" indicator (◐) to make it visually obvious that the row aggregates heterogeneous state.

Section 5

Design Direction

Lifecycle Stages (canonical)

The five columns of the saturation grid are fixed and non-overridable. Definitions are the single source for "what does this word mean?" across all crew status reports.

  • Internal / MSIT Feature is enabled for Microsoft employees on Microsoft's own tenant. No external customer exposure.
  • Dogfood / TAP Feature is enabled for a small set of named external customers (Technology Adoption Program) under NDA. Telemetry flowing back.
  • Public Preview Feature is generally available behind a flight or opt-in toggle to any tenant who enables it. Documented and supported.
  • GA Worldwide Feature is on-by-default for all eligible commercial tenants across all regions. SLAs, billing, and compliance hold.
  • Retired Feature has been deprecated and removed. Row remains for historical traceability.

UX Flow

Upstream pull (hourly): Granite Peak (engineering dates) + ExP/Iris (flight saturation) + Admin Center (tenant opt-in) → Saturation data store.

Render: User loads dashboard URL → corp-net auth + AAD group check → portfolio rollup strip + filterable feature grid renders (≤1s P95).

Drill: Feature name → per-feature detail page (lifecycle history + links to Expedite scorecard, Granite Peak entry, ADO).

Maintain: DRI receives weekly "stale row" digest with a one-click "still on track" or "update status" path; bulk nudge available to GPMs.

Key Interaction Notes

  • Status filter chips at top behave as OR within the status dimension and AND with the section/search filters — matching Granite Peak Shiproom's mental model.
  • Cells with a future date are visually distinct from cells with a past date — "06/22 (planned)" reads differently from "06/22 (live)" via subtle italic styling, so leaders never confuse intent with achievement.
  • Multi-host features expand inline (chevron on the row) rather than navigating away — matches the model-availability matrix mental model from the June 15 email without forcing a context switch.
  • The portfolio rollup strip (R-11) lives directly under the header so the dashboard always answers "how saturated is the portfolio overall?" above the fold.

Edge Cases & Empty States

  • No features in filter: Empty state reads "No features match these filters" with a one-click "Clear filters" affordance.
  • Stale row (>14 days no update): Row dims slightly; an amber clock icon appears next to the DRI; tooltip explains "Last updated 17 days ago — nudge DRI?"
  • Upstream source unreachable: Cells from the failing source render with a dashed border and a tooltip "Upstream source unavailable — last good value shown, fetched [timestamp]". Banner appears above the grid linking to the source's health page.
  • Retired features: Hidden by default; toggle "Show retired" in the filters area surfaces them with the Retired strikethrough styling for historical traceability.
  • Conflicting cross-host state: Aggregated row shows ◐ "mixed" indicator + count (e.g., "Mixed across 5 hosts"). Expanding reveals exact per-host state.

Accessibility

  • Status is conveyed by icon shape + color + text label — never color alone. Screen readers announce "Public Preview, Rolling out, target July 14" rather than just a color name.
  • Full keyboard navigation: Tab moves across cells; Enter opens drill-through; Space toggles row expansion; / focuses the search box.
  • High-contrast mode preserves status differentiation through icon shape and border weight.
Section 6

Open Questions & Risks

Open Questions

Q1: Is the canonical 5-stage lifecycle (Internal/MSIT → Dogfood/TAP → Public Preview → GA Worldwide → Retired) the right granularity for every feature crew?

Some crews track sub-stages inside Public Preview (e.g., "Targeted Release" → "Standard Release") and government clouds (GCC, GCC-H, DoD) as separate audiences. Forcing one canonical set risks pushback. Owner: David Lydston (PM) with feature-crew GPMs. Current leaning: Hold the 5-stage canon firm; expose government clouds and sub-rings as columns in a separate Audience saturation sub-view (matches Granite Peak's GCC / GCC-H / DoD / MSA columns) rather than fragmenting the primary lifecycle view.

Q2: Pull-only from upstream sources, or allow direct in-grid editing by DRIs?

Pull-only is cleaner architecturally but assumes every stage has a queryable upstream signal. Some signals (e.g., "is this Tenant opt-in?") may not exist outside a PM's head. Owner: Eng partner + David Lydston. Current leaning: Hybrid — pull where signal exists, audited manual edits where it doesn't. Manual edits decay after 30 days unless re-confirmed.

Q3: Who is the system DRI?

The dashboard is cross-crew by definition — it needs a permanent owner accountable for stage definitions, upstream integrations, and adjudicating crew exceptions. Owner: Copilot Leadership staff. Current leaning: IDEAS Experimentation owns it as an extension of the existing experimentation/portfolio surface.

Q4: What is the relationship to Granite Peak Shiproom?

Granite Peak already exists and is engineering-flavored (ship dates by audience). Saturation Dashboard is leadership-flavored (lifecycle saturation). Do these stay as two surfaces, merge, or does one become a "view" inside the other? Owner: David Lydston + Granite Peak owner. Current leaning: Keep separate; Saturation Dashboard reads from Granite Peak so there is no double-entry. Granite Peak remains the engineering-truth surface; Saturation is the leadership-truth surface.

Risks

Risk 1: Data staleness erodes trust faster than the dashboard earns it

Likelihood: High · Impact: High. A leader who sees one wrong status — "feature X is GA" when it isn't — will stop trusting the surface entirely. Mitigation: (1) Aggressive freshness flagging (R-03) so stale cells are visually unmistakable; (2) Hourly pull cadence with monitoring; (3) "Last verified" timestamp on every cell on hover; (4) Pre-launch private beta with the top 5 most-skeptical GPMs to find errors before the broader audience sees them.

Risk 2: Definition drift between crews

Likelihood: Medium · Impact: High. If "Public Preview" means three different things in three crews, the dashboard hides the disagreement behind a friendly green dot. Mitigation: (1) Published canonical definitions linked from every column header (R-02); (2) Quarterly audit of crew-specific overrides (must be zero — guardrail metric); (3) Adjudication owned by IDEAS Experimentation when crews disagree.

Risk 3: Becomes another tracker no one updates

Likelihood: Medium · Impact: Medium. Status trackers historically rot when they require manual entry. Mitigation: (1) Pull-from-upstream by default (R-04) so the DRI's burden is verification, not authoring; (2) Weekly digest with one-click "still on track" path; (3) Tie one weekly leadership review's prep flow to the dashboard URL so non-use creates visible review friction.

Risk 4: Leadership over-indexes on column-completion as "shipping success"

Likelihood: Medium · Impact: Medium. Visualizing lifecycle stages makes "all green to GA" feel like the goal, even when the right call is to extend Preview or roll back. Mitigation: (1) The dashboard explicitly does not compute a "% to GA" feature-health score; (2) Drill-through (R-13) lands on the Expedite scorecard so health-of-feature signal stays with the experimentation surface; (3) Editorial framing in the dashboard header — "lifecycle saturation, not feature quality."
