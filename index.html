About this portfolio

This site collects live, clickable product specifications I've authored for the IDEAS experimentation platform. Each project is a self-contained PM artifact — problem statement, success metrics, user scenarios, designs, and risks — paired with an interactive prototype where stakeholders can experience the proposed UX rather than just read about it.

The artifacts here were drafted, prototyped, and refined with GitHub Copilot CLI in the loop end-to-end. They reflect how I work as an AI-powered Principal PM: faster from problem to convincing artifact, with higher density of decision-quality detail per page.

Product Spec · Prototype

Expedite — Experiment Detail Page

A decision-grade view for ship / iterate / stop calls

A decision-grade detail view that turns dense experimentation scorecards into glanceable ship/iterate/stop calls. The page surfaces an advisory Copilot recommendation, the owner's actual decision, and the guardrail evidence behind it — on a single URL. A structured exception flow captures rationale when owners ship over a failed guardrail, producing a fully auditable record of every call. The result: faster, more consistent decisions across the M365 Copilot portfolio and a single source of truth for "is this ready to ship, and why?" Replaces ad-hoc scorecard reading and email threads as the deciding artifact for experiment owners and leadership.

Product Spec · Prototype

Copilot Metric Impact Page

A metric-first lens on the experiment portfolio

A metric-first lens for the Expedite experimentation platform that answers an executive's most common question: "Which experiments moved this metric?" Leaders pick a Copilot business metric — like Chat Seen WAU — apply ring and audience filters, and instantly see a ranked, statistically rigorous chart of every experiment that improved or degraded it. Drill-through links route to full scorecards, making it trivial to recognize a metric win or investigate a regression. The view flips the existing experiment-centric Impact page to a metric-centric perspective, replacing manual cross-referencing of scorecards with an instant, filterable, leadership-ready answer about portfolio-level impact on key business outcomes.

Product Spec · Prototype

One-off Scorecard

Self-service custom-window scorecard requests

A self-service drawer inside Expedite that lets experiment owners request a scorecard run over any UTC time window — without filing a DS ticket or writing SQL. Owners pick an existing scorecard, an assignment unit (User Id, Tenant Id, or Device Id), and a custom start/end, and the request appears as a "Requested" row in the rollout's existing scorecard table. On completion it flips to "Completed" and links into the same scorecard surface as cadence runs. The result: hours-not-days turnaround on custom-window analysis, DS partners freed from operational work, and every one-off captured as a first-class rollout artifact.

Product Spec

Guardrail Decision Framework

A deterministic 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴 verdict for every guardrail

A guardrails-only decision framework that turns raw scorecard confidence intervals and power into a deterministic Red / Yellow / Green verdict for every guardrail metric. Standardizes how teams interpret stat-sig vs. underpowered results, applies CI-based safety checks (CI upper bound below +Δ for harm-direction metrics; CI lower bound above −Δ otherwise), handles the "no guardrails configured" empty state, and captures rationale for every override in a fully auditable record. Replaces inconsistent team-by-team judgment with a single shared rule dictionary so the same scorecard yields the same verdict across PMs, DS, and leadership reviews.
