About this portfolio

This site collects live, clickable product specifications I've authored for the IDEAS experimentation platform. Each project is a self-contained PM artifact — problem statement, success metrics, user scenarios, designs, and risks — paired with an interactive prototype where stakeholders can experience the proposed UX rather than just read about it.

The artifacts here were drafted, prototyped, and refined with GitHub Copilot CLI in the loop end-to-end. They reflect how I work as an AI-powered Principal PM: faster from problem to convincing artifact, with higher density of decision-quality detail per page.

Product Spec

Guardrail Decision Framework

A deterministic safe / extend / not-safe verdict for every guardrail

A guardrails-only decision framework that turns raw scorecard confidence intervals and pre-experiment power into a deterministic per-guardrail verdict — safe, extend, or not-safe — and a single scorecard-level roll-up of Ship, Hold, or Stop. Each guardrail declares a direction (higher-is-better or lower-is-better) and a practical-significance threshold Δ; the framework runs a CI-based safety check (CI upper bound below +Δ for lower-is-better metrics; CI lower bound above −Δ otherwise), classifies powered vs. underpowered against MDE at 80% power, and treats underpowered guardrails as genuinely uninformative — closing the gap that today lets ~11% of experiments ship on signals that are statistically uninformative on harm. Multi-variant experiments are evaluated per T/C cell, every verdict carries a single-line reason, and any owner override of a not-safe ruling requires a written justification, mitigation, and follow-up captured in an auditable record. Replaces inconsistent team-by-team judgment with a single shared rule dictionary so the same scorecard yields the same verdict across PMs, DS, and leadership reviews.

Closing the loop from not-safe verdict to FC ownership

A mini spec that wires the Guardrail Decision Framework's verdicts directly into Feature Crew accountability. On every scorecard cadence run, any not-safe or extend verdict files an ADO bug to the owning FC within 5 minutes; Sev≤2 guardrails (reliability, safety, compliance) additionally fire IcM to the FC's on-call. Closes the gap between detection and action: today, not-safe verdicts can sit unread in scorecards; with this, an auditable, severity-mapped artifact lands in the FC's queue and on the on-call's pager within minutes of detection.

Product Spec

Anytime-Valid Inference for Experiments

Continuous-monitoring safety detection for online A/B tests

An always-on safety layer for online A/B experimentation that lets release engineers, PMs, and data scientists watch a live experiment continuously without paying the classic peeking penalty — where naive repeated looks push false-positive rates from 5% to ~70%. Built on closed-form confidence sequences (Lindon, Ham, Tingley & Bojinov 2024) and a multinomial sequential SRM test (Lindon & Malek, NeurIPS 2022), it computes Type-I-controlled bounds at every sample size from the same regression tables an experimentation platform already produces. A near-real-time data path modeled on a NRT Service feeds a shrinking-band dashboard and ICM/Geneva alerts, turning every ramped flight into a continuously-monitored canary — targeting time-to-detect under 15 minutes (vs. ~14 hours today) and 40–50% sample-size savings, with mandatory 90-day shadow-mode validation per tenant.

Mini Spec

Next-Iteration Recommender for Expedite

Given the inferred flag description and the experiment results, propose what V2 should change

A mini spec that closes the regression-to-iteration loop. When a Guardrail Decision Framework verdict returns not-safe, the recommender reads three inputs — the inferred flag description from Feature Flag Insights, the full scorecard with regression detail, and a retrieval over the 8,000+ experiment Copilot portfolio archive — and proposes a ranked 2–5 candidate list of concrete next-iteration changes. Each recommendation carries a hypothesis, change description, top-3 analog past experiments, expected impact direction, suggested guardrail to watch, and a confidence label. Owners Try this (creates a pre-populated follow-up flight shell), Modify, or Reject with reason. Aims to cut median time-from-regression-to-follow-up from ~38 days to ≤14 and lift the targeted-follow-up rate from ~12% to ≥40%.

Targeted Scorecarding in Expedite

Metadata-driven scorecard selection that replaces blanket per-product scorecards

A mini spec that replaces blanket per-product scorecards with a metadata-driven targeted set. When an experiment registers in Growth Orchestrator, IDEAS Expedite consumes its metadata — initiative_classification, surface, intervention_area, audience_tier, hypothesis — and looks up a deterministic, IDEAS-DS-owned rule dictionary (modeled on the Guardrail Decision Framework's) to pre-bind the small set of scorecards that actually measure that intervention. At the configured calendar threshold past full ramp (default 7 days; per-family configurable), only the targeted set executes. Replaces today's 15–25 mostly-flat scorecards per experiment with 3–7 meaningful reads, targeting a ≥40% reduction in scorecard count, ≥30% compute-spend savings, and missed-impact rate held under 5% via quarterly DS audits.

Self-Service Flight Telemetry Onboarding Agent

Agentic CLI that onboards partner-team flight telemetry from Cosmos to xcard without IDEAS Exp engineers in the critical path

A full 6-section product spec for a self-service onboarding agent that lets internal Microsoft partner teams move their flight telemetry — who was in which experiment condition (treatment or control) since when — from their source system (typically Cosmos) into the IDEAS Exp experiment scorecard (xcard) pipeline. Target: 70% of new onboardings completed self-service within 6 months of GA, with guardrails on scorecard validity and compute cost.
